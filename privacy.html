Geolocation.  We collect your unique user identifier and your location through GPS, WiFi, or wireless network triangulation in order to obtain your location for the purposes of providing our Service. Your exact location is not provided to other users, only that you are within a certain distance (selected by you). You set your own “proximity distance”, a radius within which friends can see you, and you them. For example, you could set your distance to 50 miles so that friends can see that you are somewhere in the New York area. Alternatively, you can set it to 0.5 miles so that your friends will know you’re in the immediate vicinity. We collect your location and locations you’re planning to visit to provide alerts when friends are, or are planning to be, within your specified proximity distance. If you turn off our application, we will not share your location with friends. We maintain location information only so long as is reasonable to provide the Service and then delete location data tied to your personal information. We may maintain aggregated or anonymized location information in order to analyze aggregate trends and metrics. Please see “Your Choices About Your Information” below to learn more.
